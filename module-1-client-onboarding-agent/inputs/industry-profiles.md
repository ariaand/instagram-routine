# Industry Profiles

**Module 1 input — Balance Operations Co. AI Bookkeeper Toolkit™**

**How to use:** Find your client's industry below and paste the matching block into
the Client Onboarding Agent alongside the `INDUSTRY TYPE` input. This sharpens the
risk assessment and cleanup plan with the issues that actually matter for that
industry. If your client's industry isn't listed, use the closest match or the
"Generic service business" profile and tell the Agent what's different.

> These are common bookkeeping considerations, not tax advice. Tax treatment and
> compliance specifics should be confirmed with the client's CPA.

---

## Restaurant / café / food service
- High cash and tip volume — watch tip reporting, cash sales recording, and undeposited funds.
- POS (Toast/Square/Clover) payouts are net of fees — gross sales vs. net deposits must be reconciled.
- Sales tax on prepared food; possible local/meals taxes.
- High COGS (food & beverage); inventory may or may not be tracked.
- Payroll-heavy with tipped wages, possible tip credits.
- Common mess: deposits booked as income without splitting out fees, sales tax, and tips.

## E-commerce / online retail
- Platform payouts (Shopify/Amazon/Stripe/PayPal) are net of fees, refunds, chargebacks — reconcile to gross.
- **Sales tax nexus** across multiple states is a major risk — confirm where they're registered.
- Inventory accounting (COGS timing) and merchant fees.
- Marketplace facilitator tax (Amazon/eBay collect for you in some states).
- Common mess: each payout booked as a single income line; sales tax and fees buried.

## Construction / trades / contractors
- Job costing and project profitability often required.
- Progress billing, retainage, and deposits/customer prepayments.
- Heavy 1099 subcontractor activity — 1099 compliance risk.
- Possible percentage-of-completion vs. completed-contract considerations (CPA call).
- Equipment, vehicles, and fixed-asset/depreciation tracking.
- Common mess: subcontractor payments uncategorized; jobs not tracked; deposits booked as income.

## Professional services / agencies / consultants
- Mostly accrual-friendly; AR aging and collections matter.
- Project/retainer billing; unearned revenue if prepaid.
- Reimbursable expenses and pass-through costs.
- Contractor-heavy — 1099s.
- Common mess: retainers booked as income immediately; reimbursables mixed with expenses.

## Real estate / property management
- Multiple properties/entities — class or location tracking is key.
- Security deposits are liabilities, not income.
- Owner draws/distributions and intercompany transfers.
- Trust/escrow accounts may need separate handling.
- Common mess: deposits booked as income; properties not separated; commingled owner funds.

## Health & wellness (salons, gyms, clinics, med-spas)
- Membership/recurring revenue and prepaid packages (deferred revenue).
- Product retail sales + service revenue + sales tax on products.
- Booth-renter vs. employee classification.
- Common mess: prepaid packages booked as income up front; product vs. service revenue not split.

## Nonprofit
- Fund/restricted vs. unrestricted accounting.
- Grants and donor restrictions; statement of functional expenses.
- Different reporting (Form 990) — coordinate with CPA.
- Common mess: restricted funds not tracked; in-kind donations missed.

## SaaS / subscription / software
- Deferred revenue and MRR/ARR recognition over the subscription period.
- Stripe/payment-processor reconciliation (fees, refunds, failed charges).
- High R&D/contractor spend.
- Common mess: annual subscriptions recognized all at once; processor fees netted into revenue.

## Generic service business (fallback)
- Reconcile all bank/credit-card accounts; clean chart of accounts.
- AR/AP aging accuracy; owner draws vs. expenses.
- 1099 contractor review; sales tax only if selling taxable goods/services.
- Confirm cash vs. accrual basis matches how the owner thinks about the business.
