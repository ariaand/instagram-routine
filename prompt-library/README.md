## What this is

A copy-paste library of **50 battle-tested prompts** for the day-to-day work of running a
QuickBooks Online (QBO) bookkeeping firm. Open a file, find the job you're doing, copy the
prompt into Claude or ChatGPT, swap in your details, and go.

**How this differs from the module agents.** The six module agents are *full workflows* —
you load one, feed it structured inputs, and it runs an entire process end to end (a complete
month-end close, a cleanup project, a proposal). This library is the opposite: **quick,
single-task jobs.** One prompt, one answer, thirty seconds. Use the agents when you're running
the whole engagement; reach for the library when you just need to categorize a stack of
transactions, draft one email, or spit out a LinkedIn post between client calls.

## How to use the library

1. **Find your task.** Browse the five category files below (see the table of contents).
2. **Copy the whole prompt** from inside the ```` ```text ```` code block.
3. **Fill in the `{{PLACEHOLDERS}}`.** Anything wrapped in double curly braces is something
   *you* supply. Two kinds:
   - **Detail placeholders** like `{{INDUSTRY}}`, `{{MONTH}}`, `{{CLIENT_NAME}}`,
     `{{HOURLY_RATE}}` — replace with a word or number.
   - **Paste placeholders** like `{{PASTE TRANSACTIONS}}` or `{{PASTE P&L}}` — delete the
     placeholder and paste the actual (anonymized) data in its place.
4. **Paste into Claude or ChatGPT** and send.
5. **Review the output.** You are the professional. The AI drafts and suggests; you verify
   and decide.

## Tips for better output

- **Give context.** The more the AI knows — industry, accounting basis (cash vs accrual),
  client size, your firm's tone — the better the answer. Most prompts have placeholders for
  exactly this. Fill them in.
- **Paste examples.** Want emails in your voice? Paste one you've already sent and say "match
  this tone." Want a chart of accounts in your standard format? Paste a sample.
- **Iterate.** The first answer is a draft. Reply with "make it shorter," "more formal,"
  "add a line about the late fee," or "you missed the sales tax accrual." Conversation beats
  one-shot.
- **Anonymize your data first** (see below).
- **Tell it the output format you want** — a table, a checklist, a bulleted email, three
  options. Many prompts already do this; adjust to taste.

## Privacy reminder (read this)

You handle other people's financial data. Protect it.

- **Anonymize before you paste.** Replace real client and customer names with `Client A`,
  `Vendor 1`, etc. The AI doesn't need the real name to categorize a transaction.
- **Never paste** full Social Security numbers, EINs, full bank/credit-card account numbers,
  routing numbers, passwords, or login credentials. Truncate (`...1234`) or omit entirely.
- **Summarize, don't dump.** You rarely need to paste the entire general ledger. Paste the
  slice that's relevant to the question.
- **Tax and legal questions get flagged, not answered.** The AI can help you draft, organize,
  and explain — but entity-level tax positions, nexus, worker classification, and legal
  exposure go to the client's **CPA or attorney.** When in doubt, say so in writing.

The AI is a drafting and analysis assistant. **You** are the bookkeeper of record.
