# SOP: Monthly Client Reporting & Communication

| | |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Deliver a consistent, accurate monthly reporting package with a plain-language summary, after the close is reviewed, so the client understands their numbers and knows what (if anything) is needed from them. |
| **Owner / Role** | {{Senior Bookkeeper / Account Manager}} |
| **Frequency** | Monthly, within {{2 business days}} of close sign-off |
| **Tools** | QBO (Reports), {{PORTAL}}, {{PM_TOOL}}, email/report template, client profile |
| **Estimated time** | 45–90 minutes per client |
| **Version** | 1.0 |

## Overview
This procedure turns a finished close into a clear deliverable. It standardizes
which reports go out, how they're reviewed for sanity, how the narrative is
written in plain English, and how outstanding questions are tracked — so
communication is reliable and the client always knows where things stand.

## Prerequisites
- Month-end close complete and reviewer-signed-off (SOP 04).
- Client profile specifies the report package, delivery channel, recipients, and
  any KPIs the client cares about.
- Prior-month package available for comparison and tone consistency.

## Procedure

**Phase 1 — Build the package**
1. Confirm the close is signed off and the period is locked (closing date set).
   **If** not, stop — never report on un-closed books.
2. In QBO **Reports**, generate the standard package on the client's basis (cash
   vs accrual): **Profit & Loss** (month and YTD), **Balance Sheet**, and the
   **A/R** and **A/P Aging Summaries**. Add **Statement of Cash Flows** or
   budget-vs-actual if the client profile requires it.
3. Set consistent report parameters every month: same date ranges, comparison
   columns (prior month / prior year), and accounting method, so periods are
   comparable.
4. Export to the agreed format (PDF/package) and save to the client's {{PORTAL}}
   folder and working papers.

**Phase 2 — Review for sanity**
5. Read the reports as the client would. Confirm cash ties to reconciled
   balances, the P&L has no surprise zeros or catch-all spikes, and A/R/A/P tie to
   the agings. Fix anything questionable before sending — the deliverable is the
   firm's reputation.
6. Identify the 2–4 things worth saying: notable revenue/expense changes, margin
   shifts, cash position, and anything the client asked about or should know.

**Phase 3 — Write & send**
7. Write a short plain-language summary (use the template): what happened this
   month, what changed vs last month/year, cash position, and any action items
   for the client. Avoid jargon; explain the "so what."
8. Keep it factual and within scope: report what the numbers show. Do **not**
   give tax, investment, or legal advice; for those, point to the CPA/attorney.
9. List any open questions or documents you still need from the client, with a
   due date, so next month's close isn't delayed.
10. Send via the client's preferred channel to the agreed recipients, attach/link
    the package, and log the send in {{PM_TOOL}}.

**Phase 4 — Follow up**
11. Track responses to your questions; chase outstanding items per the client's
    cadence and feed answers into the client profile and next close.
12. Capture any new recurring questions or reporting preferences back into the
    client profile so the package keeps improving.

## Quality checks
- [ ] Reported only after a signed-off, locked close.
- [ ] Standard package generated on the correct basis with consistent parameters.
- [ ] Reports sanity-checked; cash, A/R, and A/P tie to support.
- [ ] Summary is plain-language and explains key changes.
- [ ] No tax/legal/investment advice given; such items referred out.
- [ ] Open questions and needed documents listed with due dates.
- [ ] Delivery logged in {{PM_TOOL}}.

## Common issues & how to handle them
| Issue | How to handle |
|-------|---------------|
| Numbers look off at review | Don't send; return to close (SOP 04) and fix the root cause first. |
| Client asks "should I..." tax/finance questions | Answer what the numbers show; refer the decision to the CPA/advisor. |
| Client unresponsive to questions | Document the assumption used, note it in the summary, and re-ask next cycle. |
| Client wants a custom metric | Add it to the profile; build it consistently going forward. |
| Reports inconsistent month to month | Standardize date ranges/comparisons/basis in the saved customization. |

## Escalation
- Flag the firm owner when: the client is unhappy with the work, asks for
  something outside scope, repeatedly withholds information needed to close, or
  the relationship needs an owner-level conversation.
- → Refer to the CPA / advisor for: tax planning, entity or financing decisions,
  and any forward-looking financial advice. We report and explain history; we do
  not advise on tax or strategy.
