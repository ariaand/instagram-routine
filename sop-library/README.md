# SOP Library — 10 Ready-to-Adopt Bookkeeping SOPs

**Balance Operations Co. AI Bookkeeper Toolkit™ — The AI Operating System for Modern Bookkeepers**

This folder contains ten fully written Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for a
U.S. bookkeeping firm working in QuickBooks Online (QBO). They are not blank
templates — every step is written out and ready to use. You customize the
firm-specific details (marked with `{{PLACEHOLDERS}}`), then adopt them as your
authoritative "how we do it" documents.

---

## What an SOP is

An SOP is a documented, repeatable procedure: the exact steps a person follows to
do a recurring task correctly, every time, regardless of who does it. A good SOP
names the tool, the location, the decision points, and what "done right" looks
like.

## Why a bookkeeping firm needs them

- **Consistency.** Every client's books are handled the same way, so quality
  doesn't depend on who happened to do the work that week.
- **Delegation.** You can hand a task to a staff bookkeeper or contractor and
  trust the output, because the procedure — not the person — carries the
  knowledge.
- **Training.** New hires get productive in days instead of months. The SOP is
  the training curriculum.
- **Scaling.** You cannot add clients faster than you can add capacity. SOPs turn
  tacit know-how into a system you can grow without re-teaching everything.
- **Risk reduction.** Documented quality checks and escalation rules catch errors
  before they reach the client or the CPA.

## How to customize these SOPs

1. **Replace the placeholders.** Search each file for `{{` and fill in your
   specifics — for example `{{FIRM_NAME}}`, `{{PM_TOOL}}` (your project-management
   tool), `{{PORTAL}}` (your secure document portal), `{{AUTHOR}}`, role names,
   day-of-week timing, and any dollar thresholds.
2. **Confirm the QBO steps match your version.** QBO's menu labels shift over
   time and differ between Simple Start, Essentials, Plus, and Advanced. Walk one
   real client through each SOP and adjust wording to match what you actually see.
3. **Set your thresholds and SLAs.** Decide your own materiality threshold,
   client-response wait time, and turnaround commitments, then update the
   Escalation sections.
4. **Assign owners.** Put a real role (and ideally a name) in the **Owner / Role**
   row so there is no ambiguity about who runs each procedure.
5. **Version and date.** When you change a SOP, add a row to its Revision log and
   bump the version (1.0 → 1.1 for tweaks, 2.0 for a rework).

## How this relates to Module 5 (the SOP Builder Agent)

Module 5's **SOP Builder Agent** *generates new* SOPs from a workflow description
whenever you invent a process this library doesn't cover (a niche client
workflow, a new app integration, a firm-specific deliverable). This library gives
you **10 done** for the core bookkeeping workflows so you don't start from a blank
page. They share the same structure as the Agent's output template, so anything
you generate in Module 5 will slot in alongside these cleanly. Use the library as
your baseline; use the Agent to extend it.

> Tax and legal questions are out of scope for bookkeeping. Where an SOP touches
> tax treatment, entity structure, payroll tax filings, or compliance, it flags
> the item to the firm owner and, where appropriate, to the client's CPA or
> attorney.
