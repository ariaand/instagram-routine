## Overview
This procedure wraps up the fiscal year: it confirms all twelve months are closed
and reconciled, gathers and verifies year-end adjustments, files 1099s for
eligible vendors, and produces a clean, documented package for the CPA. The firm
prepares accurate books and information returns; the CPA prepares and files the
income tax return.

## Prerequisites
- All twelve months reconciled and closed (SOP 04) through year-end; or a
  cleanup (SOP 05) completed for the year.
- W-9s collected for vendors throughout the year (request any missing ones early).
- Prior-year tax return and the CPA's contact and preferred handoff format on file.

## Procedure

**Phase 1 — Confirm the year is closed**
1. Verify every month of the fiscal year is reconciled with $0.00 differences and
   the **For review** queue is empty. **If** any month is open, close it first.
2. Run full-year **P&L** and **Balance Sheet**; confirm cash ties to reconciled
   balances, A/R/A/P tie to agings, and loan/CC balances tie to year-end
   statements.
3. Confirm clearing/suspense accounts are clean: **Opening Balance Equity** $0,
   **Uncategorized** and **Ask My Accountant** empty, payroll/merchant clearing
   netted.

**Phase 2 — Year-end adjustments**
4. Book year-end adjustments per the CPA's guidance: depreciation/amortization,
   loan interest/principal true-ups, accrued items, owner contributions/draws,
   and any reclass the CPA requested last year. Document each journal entry.
5. Review owner/equity activity: confirm distributions, contributions, and
   shareholder loans are coded consistently (per entity type). Flag anything
   ambiguous to the CPA rather than deciding treatment.

**Phase 3 — 1099 preparation**
6. In QBO **Expenses → Contractors** (or **Vendors**), confirm which vendors are
   flagged **Track payments for 1099**. Verify each has a W-9 on file (legal name,
   TIN, address).
7. Run the **1099 Transaction Detail / Vendor Contact List** reports. Identify
   vendors meeting the reporting threshold (generally **$600+** for 1099-NEC
   services). Exclude payments made by credit card/third-party processors — those
   are reported on 1099-K by the processor, not by the client.
8. **If** a vendor is missing a W-9 or has an invalid TIN, request it before
   filing; flag any unresolved before the deadline.
9. Review the proposed 1099 list with the client/CPA for accuracy, then file
   (via QBO's 1099 e-file or the agreed method) and furnish copies to recipients
   by the deadline. Retain confirmations.

**Phase 4 — CPA handoff**
10. Assemble the handoff package: year-end **P&L** and **Balance Sheet**, **trial
    balance**, **general ledger**, reconciliation reports, the list of adjusting
    entries booked, the 1099 summary, loan statements, fixed-asset additions, and
    a notes memo of open questions/assumptions.
11. Provide CPA access (QBO **Accountant** invite or exported reports) and a
    cover note summarizing the year, anything unusual, and decisions you need from
    them (e.g., final depreciation, accrual treatment).
12. After the CPA finalizes, **book their adjusting/closing entries** into QBO to
    match the filed return, set the **closing date** (with password) at year-end,
    and confirm the books agree to the return. Log completion in {{PM_TOOL}}.

## Quality checks
- [ ] All twelve months reconciled and closed; full-year reports tie to support.
- [ ] Clearing/suspense accounts clean (OBE $0, uncategorized empty).
- [ ] Year-end adjustments booked per CPA guidance and documented.
- [ ] 1099 vendors identified; W-9s on file; card payments excluded.
- [ ] 1099s reviewed, filed, and furnished by the deadline; confirmations retained.
- [ ] CPA handoff package complete and access provided.
- [ ] CPA's final entries booked; books agree to the return; year locked.

## Common issues & how to handle them
| Issue | How to handle |
|-------|---------------|
| Missing W-9 / invalid TIN | Request immediately; consider backup-withholding implications — flag to CPA. |
| Vendor paid by card and check | Report only the non-card portion on the 1099; card portion is the processor's 1099-K. |
| Unsure if a vendor needs a 1099 | Confirm with the CPA; don't guess at reportability. |
| CPA's adjustments differ from books | Book the CPA's final entries so QBO matches the filed return. |
| A month won't reconcile at year-end | Resolve before handoff; don't hand the CPA un-reconciled books. |

## Escalation
- Flag the firm owner when: the year can't be reconciled before deadlines, W-9s
  are missing close to the filing date, or large unexplained year-end balances
  appear.
- → Refer to the CPA / attorney for: the income tax return, depreciation and
  accrual treatment, 1099 reportability edge cases, backup withholding, entity/
  equity treatment, and any tax filing. We prepare accurate books and information
  returns and provide documentation; the CPA decides tax treatment and files.
