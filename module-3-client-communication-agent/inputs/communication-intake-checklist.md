# Communication Intake — What to Tell the Agent

**Module 3 input — Balance Operations Co. AI Bookkeeper Toolkit™**

**How to use:** A quick reference for what to give the Client Communication Agent so the
first draft is the one you send. For routine emails, you barely need any of this — the
Agent will draft with `{{PLACEHOLDERS}}`. For sensitive or specific emails, the more
context you give, the better.

> 🔒 You never need to paste account numbers or sensitive data into an email draft —
> the Agent uses placeholders.

---

## The four things to give it

### 1. Email type / goal (always)
What does this email need to accomplish? Be specific:
- "Chase the June bank statement and one missing receipt."
- "Late-payment reminder, second notice, invoice 22 days overdue."
- "Deliver the May report and flag that profit dipped."
- "Follow up after today's discovery call and tee up a proposal."

### 2. Context (the specifics)
- Client/recipient name + relationship (new prospect, long-time client, behind on books).
- Relevant facts: amounts, dates, invoice #, which documents, what happened on the call.
- Anything you want acknowledged ("she just had a baby," "they were frustrated last month").

### 3. Tone (optional — defaults to warm-professional)
- **Friendly** — long relationship, low stakes.
- **Warm-professional** (default) — most situations.
- **Firm** — repeated non-response, final notice (still respectful, never scolding).

### 4. Constraints (optional)
- Length ("keep it to 4 sentences").
- Must-include ("mention the portal link," "give them until Friday").
- Your sign-off / firm name / brand voice.

---

## Copy-paste starter

```text
EMAIL TYPE / GOAL: {{what this email needs to do}}

CONTEXT: {{client name, relationship, amounts/dates/docs, anything to acknowledge}}

TONE: {{friendly / warm-professional / firm}}

CONSTRAINTS: {{length, must-include, deadline, sign-off, firm name}}
```

---

## Teach it your voice (do this once)

Paste two or three of your own past client emails and say:

> "This is how I write to clients — match this voice, tone, and sign-off in everything
> you draft from now on."

After that, every draft sounds like you, not like a template.

---

## Common requests that work well
- "Give me first / second / final versions of a late-payment reminder for the same invoice."
- "Make this half as long."
- "Make it warmer / more direct."
- "Turn the bullet list into two short sentences."
- "Add a line offering a quick call."
- "Rewrite this for text message instead of email."
