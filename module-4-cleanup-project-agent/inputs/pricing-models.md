# Cleanup Pricing Models

**Module 4 input — Balance Operations Co. AI Bookkeeper Toolkit™**

**How to use:** Pick the model that matches how you bill, fill in your own rates, and
paste the block into the Cleanup Project Agent as the `PRICING MODEL` input. The Agent
will build the hours estimate and then convert it to a price using your model.

> These are pricing **frameworks**, not a price list. Your market, niche, and
> experience set your numbers. The Agent estimates; **you set the final price.**

---

## A reliable default: estimate hours, then price the value

Most accurate cleanups start by estimating hours (the Agent does this in Deliverable 4),
then converting to a price. The conversion is where the model matters.

---

## Model 1 — Hourly (simplest, but caps your upside)

```text
PRICING MODEL: Hourly. My rate is $75/hr. Quote a range, not a fixed fee. Add a 25%
buffer to the high end for cleanup uncertainty. Require a deposit of 10 hours up front.
```

- **Pros:** safe, easy. **Cons:** punishes your speed; clients fear open-ended bills.
- Best when the scope is genuinely unknown until you're inside the books.

## Model 2 — Fixed fee from estimated hours (recommended for most cleanups)

```text
PRICING MODEL: Fixed fee derived from estimated hours × $75/hr effective target,
billed as a flat project price. Bill in phases: 50% deposit, 50% on completion (or
per-phase for projects over $2,500). Include a change-order clause if actual volume
exceeds the stated metrics by >15%.
```

- **Pros:** client gets certainty; you keep the upside if you're efficient.
- **Cons:** you eat overages if you under-scope — hence the change-order clause and
  the Agent's high-end uplift.

## Model 3 — Per-account / per-month (fast to quote, scales with the real driver)

```text
PRICING MODEL: Per-account-per-month. $35 per account per month of cleanup, with a
$500 project minimum. (E.g., 2 accounts × 12 months = $840.) Add complexity add-ons:
payroll mapping +$300, sales tax tie-out +$250, 1099 review +$150, COA rebuild +$200,
class/location setup +$200. Quote as a fixed fee with a 15% change-order threshold.
```

- **Pros:** ties price to the #1 cost driver (accounts × months); quick and defensible.
- **Cons:** needs add-ons to capture complexity (built in above).

## Model 4 — Tiered packages (best for the 3-option proposal)

```text
PRICING MODEL: Tiered fixed-fee packages built from the hours estimate:
- Essentials: core reconciliation + categorization through end date.
- Recommended: Essentials + payroll/sales-tax tie-out + balance-sheet tie-out + a
  clean first month and CPA-ready handoff.
- Done-for-You: Recommended + ongoing monthly bookkeeping at $______/mo starting after
  cleanup (and roll part of the cleanup fee into the monthly if they commit 12 months).
Target effective rate $75/hr. Quote each as a flat fee.
```

- **Pros:** anchors price, raises average deal size, bridges into recurring revenue.
- Pairs naturally with the Agent's Deliverable 2 (which already presents three options).

---

## Guardrails to give the Agent (recommended for any model)

```text
GUARDRAILS:
- Quote a range until all bank/credit-card statements are received; firm it up after.
- Never quote a single flat number for >12 months of backlog without statement review.
- Apply a +20–35% uplift to the high end for cleanup uncertainty.
- Always include a change-order trigger (volume exceeds stated metrics by >15%).
- Always take a deposit; bill in phases for projects over $2,500.
- State the effective hourly rate the recommended price implies, so I can sanity-check it.
```

---

## Sanity-check benchmarks (very rough — adjust to your market)
| Cleanup size | Rough range |
|--------------|-------------|
| Light (1 acct, 3–6 mo, low volume) | a few hundred $ |
| Typical (2 accts, ~12 mo, payroll + sales tax) | low four figures |
| Heavy (3+ accts, 18+ mo, multi-entity / inventory) | mid four figures+ |

Use these only to catch an estimate that's wildly off — not as the price itself.
