# Bookkeeper Content Engine

**Bonus — Balance Operations Co. AI Bookkeeper Toolkit™**

## What this is / who it's for

A marketing content system for bookkeepers who want a steady flow of social and email
content without staring at a blank screen. It's a strategy plus a set of copy-paste AI
prompts that turn your expertise into Instagram posts, carousels, LinkedIn content, and
email newsletters — in your voice, for your niche.

> Pairs with **Module 3 (Client Communication Agent)**: same "AI drafts, you review and
> post" discipline, pointed outward at marketing instead of client emails.

---

## Part 1 — Content strategy (the foundation)

### Your content pillars (rotate through these)
1. **Educate** — teach one useful thing (a deduction, a QBO tip, a deadline). Builds trust.
2. **Myth-bust / mistakes** — "3 bookkeeping mistakes costing you money." Highly shareable.
3. **Behind the scenes** — how you work, a cleanup before/after, your process. Builds connection.
4. **Proof / results** — client wins, testimonials, transformations (anonymized).
5. **Personality / story** — why you do this, your values. People hire people.
6. **Offer / CTA** — what you sell and how to start. ~1 in 5 posts.

### Cadence (sustainable beats heroic)
- **Instagram:** 3×/week (mix of single posts + 1 carousel).
- **LinkedIn:** 2–3×/week (text posts; repurpose your best IG ideas).
- **Email newsletter:** 1×/month minimum (2× ideal). Your owned audience — most valuable.

### Voice
Pick 3 adjectives for your brand voice (e.g., "approachable, sharp, no-nonsense") and feed
them to every prompt. Consistency is what makes content recognizably *yours*.

## Part 2 — The prompts

> Set `{{NICHE}}` (e.g., "restaurants," "creative agencies," "trades"), `{{FIRM_NAME}}`,
> and `{{VOICE}}` once and reuse them everywhere.

### Instagram — single post
```text
You are a social media copywriter for a bookkeeping firm ({{FIRM_NAME}}) that serves
{{NICHE}}. Voice: {{VOICE}}. Write an Instagram single-post caption that {{EDUCATES on /
busts the myth that / shares the lesson}}: "{{TOPIC}}".
Structure: a scroll-stopping first line (hook), 3–6 short lines of value, one clear CTA.
Add a tasteful emoji or two, and 5–8 relevant hashtags. Keep it skimmable. Give me 2 hook
variations.
```

### Instagram — carousel (slide by slide)
```text
You are a content designer for a bookkeeping firm ({{FIRM_NAME}}) serving {{NICHE}}.
Voice: {{VOICE}}. Create an Instagram carousel on "{{TOPIC}}". Output slide-by-slide:
- Slide 1: a bold hook/title (≤8 words) that makes people swipe.
- Slides 2–7: one idea each — a short heading + 1–2 lines. Make them build logically.
- Final slide: a recap + a clear CTA ({{e.g., "DM me CLEANUP", "link in bio"}}).
Also give me the post caption and 5–8 hashtags. Keep text minimal — it's a visual format.
```

### LinkedIn — text post
```text
You are writing a LinkedIn post for the owner of a bookkeeping firm ({{FIRM_NAME}})
serving {{NICHE}}. Voice: {{VOICE}}, professional but human. Topic: "{{TOPIC}}".
Structure: a strong one-line hook, a line break, then 4–8 short punchy lines (one thought
per line, generous white space), and end with a question to drive comments. No hashtag
spam — 3 max. Give me 2 versions: one story-led, one tips-led.
```

### Email newsletter
```text
You are writing the monthly email newsletter for a bookkeeping firm ({{FIRM_NAME}})
serving {{NICHE}}. Voice: {{VOICE}}. Theme: "{{THEME}}".
Output: (1) 3 subject-line options, (2) a short, friendly newsletter (~200–300 words) with
one main tip the reader can act on this month, a quick seasonal/deadline reminder if
relevant, and a soft CTA to {{book a call / reply / refer a friend}}. Skimmable, warm, no
jargon. End with a P.S. that reinforces the CTA.
```

### Repurpose one idea into a week of content
```text
Take this one idea and turn it into a week of content for a bookkeeping firm serving
{{NICHE}} (voice: {{VOICE}}): 1 Instagram single post, 1 carousel (slide-by-slide), 1
LinkedIn post, and 1 short email. Keep the core message consistent but adapt format and
length to each platform. Idea: "{{IDEA}}".
```

### Monthly content calendar
```text
You are a content strategist for a bookkeeping firm ({{FIRM_NAME}}) serving {{NICHE}}.
Build a 4-week content calendar (3 IG + 2 LinkedIn posts per week, 1 newsletter) using
these pillars: Educate, Myth-bust, Behind-the-scenes, Proof, Personality, Offer. For each
slot give: date/day, pillar, format, and a one-line topic. Tie in any seasonal/tax
deadlines relevant to {{MONTH}}. Output as a table.
```

## Part 4 — Fully written examples

### Example: Instagram single post (Myth-bust)
> **Hook:** Your bookkeeper and your accountant are NOT the same person. 🧾
>
> Here's the difference (and why you probably need both):
>
> 📚 A bookkeeper keeps your numbers clean *all year* — categorizing, reconciling, closing
> the books every month.
>
> 🧮 An accountant uses those clean numbers *once a year* to file your taxes and advise.
>
> If your books are a mess, your accountant is doing cleanup at tax time — on the clock,
> at the worst possible moment.
>
> Clean books all year = lower tax bills, fewer surprises, and an accountant who actually
> has time to save you money.
>
> 👉 DM me "BOOKS" if tax season feels like a fire drill every year.
>
> #smallbusiness #bookkeeping #{{NICHE}} #taxtips #businessfinance

### Example: LinkedIn post (Tips-led)
> Most {{NICHE}} owners don't have a revenue problem.
>
> They have a *visibility* problem.
>
> They're profitable — they just can't see it, because the books are 3 months behind.
>
> Here's what changes when your books are current:
>
> → You spot a slow month while you can still act on it
> → You stop overdrafting because you know your real cash position
> → You make pricing decisions with data, not vibes
> → Tax season becomes a non-event
>
> Clean, current books aren't an expense. They're how you see the business clearly.
>
> What's the longest your books have ever fallen behind?

## Pro tips
- **Batch it.** Use the "week of content" or "monthly calendar" prompt once a month, then
  refine the pieces you like. One hour → a month of content.
- **Teach the AI your voice.** Paste 2–3 of your real posts and say "match this."
- **Lead with hooks.** The first line is 80% of the result — always generate 2–3 variations.
- **Reuse winners.** A post that performed well becomes a carousel, then a newsletter, then
  a reel script.
- **Always review before posting.** Check facts (especially tax deadlines/figures) and
  make sure client examples are fully anonymized.

> Nothing here is tax advice; if a post veers into tax specifics, verify and add a "talk
> to your CPA" nudge.
