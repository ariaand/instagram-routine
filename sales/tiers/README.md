## Why this split works

- **Starter** is the *get-clients* bundle: onboard, scope/price a cleanup, and propose a
  monthly engagement — the revenue-generating core. Low price, high perceived value, easy
  first "yes."
- **Pro** adds the *run-the-firm* layer: deliver the monthly close, communicate with
  clients, document processes, and install everything as reusable assistants. This is the
  natural home for most buyers — price it as the recommended option.
- **Premium** adds the *grow-the-firm* layer: the marketing engine, the standalone cleanup
  toolkit, the discovery-call system, plus updates. For firms ready to scale.

---

## Pricing & upgrade logic
- Anchor on **Pro** in your marketing (the table's ⭐). Starter is the entry point;
  Premium is the value anchor that makes Pro look reasonable.
- Offer **upgrade for the difference** ($97→$297, $297→$497) so early buyers move up.
- The three bonus toolkits double as **standalone mini-products** or **order bumps** if you
  prefer to sell them à la carte.
