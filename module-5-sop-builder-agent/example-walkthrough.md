# Example Walkthrough — Weekly Categorization → SOP, Checklist, Training Doc

**Module 5 — Balance Operations Co. AI Bookkeeper Toolkit™**

A bookkeeper (Aria, of the fictional Balance Operations Co.) brain-dumps her weekly
categorization workflow and gets back three polished documents. Abbreviated for length.

## The input we gave the Agent

```text
WORKFLOW: Weekly categorization. Every Friday I go through each client's bank feed in
QBO. Most transactions auto-match from bank rules. The rest I categorize by hand. If I
don't know what something is I leave it in Ask My Accountant and add it to my client
question list. I check for duplicates and make sure transfers between accounts aren't
counted as income or expense. When the feed is clear I mark the client done in ClickUp.

TOOLS: QBO, bank feeds/rules, client tracker in ClickUp, client portal for questions.
ROLE & FREQUENCY: Me (and soon a new staff bookkeeper). Weekly, every Friday.
RULES/STANDARDS: Never guess on anything over $500 — always ask. Feed cleared by EOD Friday.
```

Rules named "Vendor – Category." Questions go in a running portal thread per client.

## Document 1 — SOP (abbreviated)

**SOP: Weekly Transaction Categorization**
*Purpose: keep every client's books current and accurate so month-end close is fast.
Owner: Staff Bookkeeper. Frequency: Weekly (Fri). Tools: QBO, bank feeds, ClickUp,
portal. Est. time: 20–40 min/client. v1.0.*

**Procedure**
1. Open client QBO → Transactions → Bank feed. Confirm the feed is current.
2. Review auto-matched items from bank rules; accept if correct.
3. Categorize remaining transactions. If a vendor is new, create a bank rule
   ("Vendor – Category").
4. **Decision:** if a transaction is **>$500 and unclear**, do NOT guess — park in Ask My
   Accountant and add to the client's portal question thread.
5. Book owner personal spend to **Owner's Draw** (equity), never to an expense.
6. Verify transfers between accounts are categorized as transfers, not income/expense.
7. Scan for duplicates; verify against the statement before deleting.
8. Quality check (below). When clean, mark the client **Done** in ClickUp.

**Quality checks:** zero uncategorized (except intentional AMA) · no duplicates ·
transfers correct · owner draws correct · questions sent.

**Escalation:** unresolved discrepancy or unresponsive client >5 days → firm owner.
→ Refer tax-treatment questions to CPA.

**Revision log:** 2026-06-15 · v1.0 · Initial SOP · Aria.

## Document 3 — Training Document (abbreviated)

**Training: Weekly Transaction Categorization**

*What it is:* every Friday you sort each client's bank and card activity into the right
categories in QBO.

*Why it matters:* this is the foundation of clean books. If categorization is accurate
and current, month-end close is fast and the client's reports are trustworthy. If it
slips, errors compound and everyone's week gets harder.

**Walk-through (with the why):**
- **Accept auto-matches** — *why:* bank rules do 80% of the work, but they're not
  perfect. ⚠️ *Watch out:* a rule can mis-categorize a vendor; glance before accepting.
- **Park unknowns over $500** — *why:* a wrong guess on a big number distorts the P&L.
  💡 *Tip:* it's always better to ask than to guess.
- **Owner's Draw, not expense** — *why:* personal spending isn't a business expense;
  miscategorizing it overstates costs and understates owner equity.

**Most common mistakes:** guessing instead of asking · booking transfers as income ·
forgetting to mark Done so the team loses track of status.

**You're doing it right when:** zero stray uncategorized items, no dupes, transfers and
draws correct, questions sent. **Ask for help when:** unsure on anything >$500, or it
won't reconcile after a few minutes.
